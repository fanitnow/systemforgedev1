Section 02

Outreach scripts

Use these word-for-word. Do not improvise until you've sent each one at least 10 times. The language is designed to get a response, not to impress.

INSTAGRAM DM Cold Opener — Works on Any Business
▼
Quick question — do you have anything set up to automatically follow up with people who reach out but don't book? I help [barbershops / trainers / businesses like yours] find and fix the spots where they're losing customers. Takes about 7 minutes to run through and I can show you exactly where the gaps are. Would it be okay if I sent you a quick audit? No pitch, just the info.
Copy
Send this cold. Don't introduce yourself yet. Don't explain what you do. Just ask the one question that makes them think. If they respond, you have them.
INSTAGRAM DM Follow-Up After No Response (Day 3)
▼
Hey [Name] — not trying to be annoying, just wanted to drop this here. I ran a quick look at businesses in your space and most of them are losing between $2K–$5K a month from the same 3 things: - No automated follow-up - Booking friction - No system to bring past clients back If any of those hit close to home, I can show you exactly what's happening in your business specifically. Free, no strings. Just reply "yes" if you want me to take a look.
Copy
The specific dollar number creates urgency. The "just reply yes" makes response frictionless. Only send this once — if no response after this, move on.
IN PERSON / TEXT Local Business Walk-In Approach
▼
Hey — I work with businesses on their backend systems. Quick question: when someone calls or DMs you and doesn't book, what happens after that? [Let them answer] That's actually the most common place businesses lose money — there's no system to follow up automatically. I built a tool that audits exactly where that's happening and what it costs. Takes 7 minutes. Want me to run it on your business right now?
Copy
Walk-ins are your fastest path to money. Barbershops, salons, auto shops, gyms. Show up during a slow period. Ask the question. Let them talk. The audit is your close tool.
EMAIL Cold Email to Small Business Owner
▼
Subject: Quick question about [Business Name] Hi [Name], I came across [Business Name] and had a quick question: is there currently a system in place to automatically follow up with customers who reach out but don't book? I run SystemForge AI — we help small businesses find and plug revenue leaks in their operations. Most businesses we work with are losing $2,000–$6,000 a month from 3–4 fixable gaps in their systems. I can run a quick audit (7 minutes) and show you exactly what's happening in your business — no pitch, just the findings. Worth a look? — [Your Name] SystemForge AI
Copy
Short subject, one clear question, no buzzwords. The goal is a "yes" reply, not to close in the email. Reply rate improves significantly when you personalize the business name.
AFTER AUDIT Script to Close the $750 Package
▼
"Based on what you showed me, you've got three main gaps: no lead capture, no automated follow-up, and your booking flow is losing people before they commit. Conservative estimate — that's probably $2,500–$4,000 a month walking out the door. I can fix all three of those this week. Lead capture, 5-touch follow-up sequence, and a proper booking flow — all set up and working. It's $750, one time, and I'll have it running within 48 hours. Want to do it?"
Copy
Say the number out loud. Don't flinch. Don't apologize for the price. After you say "Want to do it?" — stop talking. The first person who speaks loses. Let the silence work.

Section 04

Handle every objection

Every objection is a request for more information. Know these cold. Practice them out loud before your first call.

✗"I don't have the budget right now."
▼
"I hear you — that's actually exactly why I'm here. Based on what you've described, you're losing roughly $2,000–$3,000 a month right now from leads that never got followed up. The fix costs $750 one time. You'll make that back in the first week it's running. The question is whether you can afford to keep losing what you're losing."
✗"I need to think about it."
▼
"Of course — what specifically do you need to think through? I want to make sure you have everything to make the decision. Is it the price, the timing, or whether this will actually work for your specific situation?"
✗"I already have [Calendly / Square / Instagram]."
▼
"Good — those are tools. What I'm building is the system that connects them and makes them work together automatically. Calendly sends a booking confirmation. We build the follow-up sequence that goes out before and after. Most businesses have the tools — they just don't have them wired together."
✗"I don't know if this will work for my business."
▼
"That's fair — which part are you unsure about? The audit I ran showed you're missing [X and Y]. Both of those are the same gaps in every business I've worked with. They're not unique to you — and neither are the fixes. That's why I can build them in 48 hours."
✗"Can you do it cheaper?"
▼
"I can't discount the build, but here's what I can do — start with the $97 Audit Report today. You get the full diagnosis and roadmap. If you move forward with the $750 build within 7 days, I'll apply the $97 as a credit. So you'd only pay $653 for the full system."
Section 05

Closing the deal

The audit does most of the work. By the time you present findings, they already know something is broken. Your job is to make the decision easy.

SituationWhat to say
They're nodding along to audit findings "I can fix all of this. I'll have it running within 48 hours. The System Starter is $750 — want to move forward today?"
They ask "How long does it take?" "48 hours from when you complete intake. Most clients have everything live before the weekend."
They're a larger business or want everything "Given the size of what you're running, the Full Build makes more sense — that's $2,500 and we build everything end to end in about a week. Want me to scope it out?"
They're hesitant but interested "Let's start with the $97 Audit Report. No commitment, just clarity. If you love it, we'll move forward."
They want to know the guarantee "If I don't deliver within the timeframe, full refund. No questions. And I'll give you a 7-day check-in call to make sure everything's working."
Never say these things: "It's a great deal," "You won't regret it," "Trust me," or "I'll give you a discount." These kill confidence. Say the price once, clearly, then stop talking.
